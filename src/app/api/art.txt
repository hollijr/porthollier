appleblossom cane:
<p>These instructions are for the auction project.</p>
<p>Materials:&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 8.5pt; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">1 - 1" ball for inner petals<br /> 1 - 1/2" x 2" outer vein strip<br /> 1 - 1/2" x 1" inner vein strip<br /> 1 - 9" x 1" flat Skinner blend for outer petals<br /> 1 - 4" x 1" flat outline strip<br /> 1 - 3/8" ball for center</span></p>
<table border="0" cellpadding="5" align="left" style="width: 600px;">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm1.jpg" width="300" height="233" alt="appblossm1" />&nbsp;</td>
<td>&nbsp;1. &nbsp;Roll the inner petal ball into a 1.75-inch long round log.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm2.jpg" width="300" height="306" alt="appblossm2" /></td>
<td>&nbsp;2. &nbsp;Shape the log into a 2" long teardrop shape.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm3.jpg" width="300" height="302" alt="appblossm3" /></td>
<td>&nbsp;3. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">Place the 1/2" x 2" outer vein strip on the log so that one of its long sides is parallel to and extends about 1/8" past the pinched side of the log.</span></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm4.jpg" width="300" height="253" alt="appblossm4" /></td>
<td>&nbsp;4. &nbsp;Slice the log in half widthwise. &nbsp;Each half should measure about 1" long.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm5.jpg" width="300" height="288" alt="appblossm5" /></td>
<td>&nbsp;5. &nbsp;Turn one half around so the veined side points away from the other. &nbsp;Add the 1/2" x 1" &nbsp;inner vein strip along the flat side of one of the halves, aligning the lower edges of the inner and outer veins (i.e., 1/8" past the bottom edge of the half).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm6.jpg" width="300" height="309" alt="appblossm6" /></td>
<td>&nbsp;6. &nbsp;Place the other half against the inner vein, having flat sides together and making sure the bottom edges of the veins strips are aligned. &nbsp;Gently compress to make sure the pieces adhere. &nbsp;This is the inner petal log. &nbsp;It should be approximately 1" long.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<p>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm7.jpg" width="300" height="302" alt="appblossm7" /></p>
<p><img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm8.jpg" width="300" height="299" alt="appblossm8" /></p>
</td>
<td>
<p>&nbsp;7. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">Place the lighter-colored (1" wide) end of the 9" x 1" Skinner blend along the 1/8" vein extension on one side of the inner petal log. &nbsp;Offset it slightly so you can still see the bottom of the vein. &nbsp;</span> &nbsp;</p>
<p>Wrap the blend over the top of the inner petal log stopping when you reach the veined extension on the other side. &nbsp;Fold the strip back on itself in order to wrap back over the top of the log going the other way. &nbsp;(You don't want to wrap the blend under the vein extension.)</p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<p>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm9.jpg" width="300" height="305" alt="appblossm9" /></p>
<p><img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm10.jpg" width="300" height="308" alt="appblossm10" /></p>
<p><img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm11.jpg" width="300" height="199" alt="appblossm11" /></p>
</td>
<td>8. &nbsp;<span style="color: #000000;">Moving back and forth from one side of the log to the other, drape the blend over the top of the inner petal log as in step 7, but stop and turn back about 1/4" sooner than the previous turn on that side<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">. &nbsp;Continue until you reach the top and the blend runs out</span>.</span></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm13.jpg" width="300" height="309" alt="appblossm13" /></td>
<td>9. &nbsp;Gently compress the draping into the inner petal log<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">, rounding and smoothing the top and closing any gaps between layers.</span></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm14.jpg" width="300" height="315" alt="appblossm14" /></td>
<td>10. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">Wrap the 4" x 1" border strip around the petal log.</span></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm15.jpg" width="300" height="304" alt="appblossm15" /></td>
<td>
<p>11. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">Elongate (or reduce*) the petal log to 7.5 inches.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 9pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; color: black;">*To reduce: &nbsp;squeeze along the wrapped sides of the log, starting at the midpoint of its length and working your way towards the ends, until the clay forms a long log. &nbsp;<b>Do not</b>&nbsp;roll the clay to lengthen it.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 9pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; color: black;"><span style="font-size: 9pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; color: black;">For more information about reducing,&nbsp;</span><span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;"><a href="http://www.polymerclaycentral.com/poleigh/reducing.html" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: 9pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; color: #2e8fd4; border: 1pt none windowtext; padding: 0in;">click here</span></a></span><span style="font-size: 9pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Helvetica, sans-serif; color: black;">.</span></span></p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm16.jpg" width="300" height="296" alt="appblossm16" /></td>
<td>12. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">As you lengthen it, pinch along the veined side of the log to form a wide teardrop shape.</span> &nbsp;</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm17.jpg" width="300" height="156" alt="appblossm17" /></td>
<td>13. &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 8.5pt; line-height: 115%; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; color: black;">Cut the elongated log into five 1.5" pieces.</span></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm18.jpg" width="300" height="271" alt="appblossm18" /></td>
<td>14. &nbsp;Roll the 3/8" center ball into a 1.5-inch rope.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm19.jpg" width="300" height="280" alt="appblossm19" /></td>
<td>15. &nbsp;Arrange the petal pieces around the center rope having the vein/pinched edge against the center.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>&nbsp;<img src="images/clay/appleblossom/appblossm20.jpg" width="300" height="265" alt="appblossm20" /></td>
<td>16. &nbsp;Gently press the petals toward the center until any gaps around the center are closed.</td>
</tr>
